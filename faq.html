How do I create an account?

Creating an account is simple. Download the BloodLine app from Google Play or the App Store, open the app, and tap "Sign Up". Fill in your personal details, including your name, email, phone number, blood type, and create a password. Verify your email address, and you're all set!

Can I use BloodLine without creating an account?

While you can browse some basic information without an account, creating an account is necessary to access most features of the BloodLine app, including finding donors, making blood requests, tracking donations, and managing your health profile.

How do I reset my password?

If you've forgotten your password, tap "Forgot Password" on the login screen. Enter your email address, and we'll send you a link to reset your password. Follow the instructions in the email to create a new password.

How often can I donate blood?

The recommended frequency for whole blood donation is every 8-12 weeks (56-84 days). This interval allows your body to replenish the donated blood cells. The BloodLine app will track your donations and notify you when you're eligible to donate again.

How does BloodLine verify my blood type?

While BloodLine initially relies on the blood type information you provide during registration, we encourage users to upload verification documents such as blood test reports or donor cards. For verified donors, a special badge appears on their profile, building trust in the community.

How do I track my donation history?

BloodLine provides a comprehensive donation tracking system. Navigate to the "Donation History" section in your profile, where you'll find all your past donations, including dates, locations, and status. You can also view helpful charts that visualize your donation frequency over time.

How do I create a blood request?

To create a blood request, go to the "Requests" tab and tap "Create New Request." Fill in the details including the patient's name, blood type needed, hospital location, urgency level, and any additional notes. Once submitted, the request will be visible to compatible donors in your area.

How are donors matched to my blood request?

BloodLine uses a sophisticated matching algorithm that considers blood type compatibility, donor location, availability status, and donation eligibility. When a request is created, notifications are automatically sent to the most suitable donors in the vicinity.

Can I specify the urgency of my blood request?

Yes, when creating a blood request, you can select the urgency level from "Standard" to "Emergency." Emergency requests receive priority notifications and are highlighted in the app for greater visibility to potential donors.

Is my personal information secure?

We take data security very seriously. BloodLine uses advanced encryption technologies to protect your personal information. We only share the minimal information necessary with other users for the purpose of blood donation. For complete details, please review our Privacy Policy.

How does location tracking work in the app?

BloodLine uses your location to connect you with nearby donors or blood requests. Location permissions are optional but recommended for the best experience. You can control location permissions in your device settings or within the app's privacy settings.

Does BloodLine work offline?

BloodLine requires an internet connection for most features, including finding donors, creating requests, and real-time updates. However, some basic information like your donation history and saved emergency contacts may be available offline once loaded.
